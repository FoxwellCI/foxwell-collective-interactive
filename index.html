Foxwell Collective Interactive is an independent game studio operated by two main owners, working collaboratively across all areas of development.

All creative decisions are discussed and shaped together. Narrative, gameplay, art direction and atmosphere are developed through shared iteration rather than isolated roles.

While both owners actively contribute to every part of the project, each brings stronger expertise in specific areas.

Pol, publicly known as QuietPollen, serves as the Lead Developer and focuses primarily on technical development, narrative structure, systems design and overall project coordination.

HushPetal is the second main owner and specializes in visual design, concept art, visual storytelling and maintaining the project’s aesthetic and atmospheric consistency.

Team members may use official or semi-official social media accounts to share behind-the-scenes content, development thoughts and subtle hints about future projects. These accounts do not display sensitive personal information and must follow internal studio guidelines.

Current Project

The studio currently has a narrative-driven project in active development, with its universe, characters and core gameplay concepts already defined.

The project belongs to the Mascot Horror genre, focusing on psychological tension, emotional immersion and character interaction rather than explicit violence.

The estimated release window is between 2026 and 2027, allowing adequate time for refinement, polish and creative consistency.

Studio Philosophy

Foxwell Collective Interactive believes that impactful horror emerges from atmosphere, pacing and narrative consequences rather than shock value alone.

Each project is treated as an authorial experience, where writing, visual design, sound and interactivity are developed together to support a cohesive vision.

Team Guidelines

To protect the studio’s creative integrity, trust and long-term vision, all members representing Foxwell Collective Interactive publicly must follow the guidelines below.

What team members are allowed to post

• General development updates that do not reveal confidential information.
• Behind-the-scenes content approved internally.
• Personal thoughts about game development, art or design when clearly stated as personal opinions.
• Approved artwork, screenshots or concepts that have been cleared for public use.
• Studio-related posts that align with the project’s tone and values.

What is not allowed to be posted

• Unreleased story details, endings, major plot points or twists.
• Source code, internal tools, private builds or proprietary systems.
• Private conversations, internal discussions or disagreements.
• Personal data of team members or third parties.
• Misleading statements about the project, release dates or studio decisions.
• Content involving illegal, abusive or harmful activities.

Approval and responsibility

Content that could impact the studio’s image or reveal development details must be approved internally before publication. Team members are responsible for ensuring their posts comply with these guidelines.

Consequences of violations

Violations are handled proportionally depending on severity:

• Minor issues may result in a formal warning or request to remove content.
• Repeated or serious violations may lead to temporary suspension from the team.
• Severe breaches of trust, legality or confidentiality may result in permanent removal from the studio and termination of collaboration.
